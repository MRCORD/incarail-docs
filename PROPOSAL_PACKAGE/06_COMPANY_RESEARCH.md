## Executive Summary

**TL;DR**: IncaRail (Inca Rail S.A.C.) is a Peruvian luxury train operator founded in 2007, backed by Carlyle Group since 2016, operating exclusively on the Cusco-Machu Picchu route. As the sole competitor to dominant PeruRail in a duopoly market, IncaRail serves ~450K of 1.5M annual Machu Picchu visitors. Despite premium positioning with $60-500 USD fares across four train classes and strategic Carlyle ownership, IncaRail faces mixed customer reviews, limited market share vs PeruRail's longer-established presence, and operates in a regulatory-constrained capacity-constrained duopoly with infinite demand but fixed capacity.

**Key Metrics**:
- **Founded**: 2007 (commercial operations began October 5, 2009)
- **Market Position**: #2 in duopoly (smaller than PeruRail)
- **Estimated Annual Passengers**: ~450,000
- **Employee Count**: ~202 employees (as of 2024)
- **Estimated Annual Revenue**: $17.5M USD (from public databases)
- **Price Range**: $60-500 USD per ticket (train only), $70-509 USD (packages)
- **Routes**: Exclusively Cusco/Ollantaytambo ‚Üí Machu Picchu
- **Ownership**: Carlyle Peru Fund (majority), Forsyth family (minority)
- **Customer Rating**: 2/5 on Trustpilot, Mixed on TripAdvisor (13,855 reviews)
- **Website Traffic**: 153K monthly visits (ranked #3,113 in Peru)

**CRITICAL OWNERSHIP CLARIFICATION**:

‚ùå **FALSE**: "Both companies owned by same parent" (circulated but unverified)

‚úÖ **ACTUAL OWNERSHIP** (Verified 2024):
- **PeruRail**: 50% owned by **Belmond Ltd.** (acquired by **LVMH** in 2019 for $3.2B)
  - Remaining 50%: Other shareholders (not publicly disclosed)
  - Parent company: LVMH Mo√´t Hennessy Louis Vuitton (French luxury conglomerate)
  - Part of Belmond's global luxury hotel/train portfolio (43 properties, 24 countries)
  - Includes iconic Venice Simplon-Orient-Express, Hotel das Cataratas, Mount Nelson

- **IncaRail**: Majority owned by **Carlyle Group** (U.S. private equity)
  - Minority: Forsyth family + management
  - Investment completed June 30, 2016
  - No connection to LVMH/Belmond

**Conclusion**: These are **genuinely independent competitors** with different ownership, strategies, and capital structures. PeruRail = luxury hospitality conglomerate asset, IncaRail = PE-backed standalone operator.

**Regulatory Structure**:
- 30-year concession contract to Ferrocarriles del Peru consortium
- Split into: Ferrovias Central Andina (central railroad) + Fertransa (southern routes including Machu Picchu)
- Concession expires 2034
- Only PeruRail and IncaRail have operating rights on Ollantaytambo-Aguas Calientes route
- This is **government-granted duopoly**, not monopoly
